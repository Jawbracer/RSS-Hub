Reading List

For your betterment, and continued eduction, the Party reccomends familariarsing yourself with a selection of readings

    Introduction and General Reading
  • K Marx & F. Engels, Manifesto of the Communist Party (1848), Collected Works Vol.6 (also available from the as part of the Communist Party’s Classics of Communism range, 2006)
  • David McLellan, The Thought of Karl Marx (Papermac, 1980)
  • F Engels,‘Principles of Communism’ (1847), Collected Works Vol.6
  • Mary Davis, Women and Class (Communist Party, 1999)
  • Basic Economics: A Citizen's Guide to the Economy - Thomas Sowell
    Capitalism and Exploitation
  • EK Hunt & Howard J. Sherman, Economics—An Introduction to Traditional and Radical Views (HarperCollins, 1990)
  • Jason Hickle, The Divide: Global Inequality from Conquest to Free Markets
    Africa & Blackness
  • Adi, Hakim. Pan-Africanism and Communism: The Communist International, Africa and the Diaspora, 1919-1939.
  • Hutchinson, Earl. Blacks and Reds: Race and Class in Conflict, 1919-1990.
    Central & South America
  • Guerrilla Warfare by Ernesto ‘Che’ Guevara
  • The Zapatista Reader by edited by Tom Hayden
  • Century of Revolution: Insurgent and Counterinsurgent Violence During Latin America’s Long Cold War edited by Greg Grandin and Gilbert M. Joseph
  • Salvador Allende: Revolutionary Democrat by Victor Figueroa-Clark
  • Worker Struggles that Predate the Brazilian Communist Party by Astrojildo Pereira

As we are unable to distribute books via this website, please contact a party member for information about the books and where to find them. If you feel additions or substractions should be made then please contact a party member.
